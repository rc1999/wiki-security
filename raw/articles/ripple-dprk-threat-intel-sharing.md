# Ripple Begins Sharing DPRK Threat Intel with Crypto Firms

**Published:** May 4, 2026 | **Source:** crypto.news / Crypto ISAC

## Key Points

- Ripple has begun sharing its internal North Korean threat intelligence with the crypto industry via **Crypto ISAC's new API**
- Distributed data includes: DPRK-linked wallet addresses, malicious domains, suspected operative profiles (LinkedIn IDs, emails, locations)
- Goal: Help firms detect **insider-driven infiltration** before catastrophic losses
- Total Lazarus-attributed losses in early 2026: **$577 million**
  - **Drift Protocol:** $285 million (via 6-month social engineering campaign)
  - **Kelp DAO:** $292 million
- **Coinbase** already integrated with the new Crypto ISAC API

## Threat Pattern

> "Threat actors rejected by one firm often reapply to several others the same week"

Without shared intelligence, each company starts from zero. A rejected applicant at Company A applies to Companies B, C, D the same week — each unaware of the prior rejection and enriched screening data.
